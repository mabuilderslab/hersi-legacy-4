Her Story

The Architect of Bungoma

Why This Legacy Is Preserved


This website exists to preserve the life, work, and values of Sarah Hersi Ali, known to many as Mama Hersi. Her legacy lives not in records or institutions, but in people — families she fed, women she supported, and a community she helped hold together through decades of change.


This is not a story of wealth alone, but of responsibility, courage, and service.

Who She Was

Sarah Hersi Ali was a businesswoman, community pillar, and matriarch whose work shaped everyday life in Bungoma and surrounding regions. Known for discipline, fairness, and generosity, she built businesses that served people first and profit second. Her presence was trusted across families, markets, and generations.

Chapter 1

The Roots

Before Bungoma, before New Nyanza, there was a vision taking shape. Sarah Hersi Ali came from a lineage that understood the value of trade, the power of determination, and the necessity of building something lasting.

Family stories speak of a woman who saw opportunity where others saw obstacles, who understood that true wealth was measured not just in what you accumulated, but in what you enabled others to build.

Chapter 2

1958: The Pioneer Arrives

Long before the era of modern logistics, Sarah Hersi Ali saw potential where others saw distance. In 1958, she moved her young family from Nakuru to Bungoma, a quiet outpost in Western Kenya. She didn't come to settle; she came to build.

While others traded small goods, Mama Hersi dealt in the materials of nation-building. Bungoma was a frontier—and she was its architect.

Chapter 3

The Hardware Matriarch

As one of the first major agents for Bamburi Cement in the region, she became the "Iron Lady" of Western Kenya's construction boom. From hardware to commodities like sugar and maize, she commanded a supply chain that bridged the Coast to the Border.

She taught us that business is not just about profit—it is about being essential.

  • Established New Nyanza Wholesalers Ltd
  • Major Bamburi Cement agent
  • Cross-border trade networks (Kenya-Uganda)
  • Property acquisitions during independence era
  • Trained next generation in accounting & business
Chapter 4

Resilience Through Adversity

After losing her husband when her daughter Amina was just nine years old, Sarah Hersi Ali became the sole provider for six children. Most would have retreated. She expanded.

She pulled her daughters into the business young. She was their first business school. She taught them that a handshake is a contract, that inventory is cash, and that resilience is the only currency that never devalues.

"She was both mother and father to us. The shop floor was our classroom."
Chapter 5

At the Helm

Today, despite the passing of years and challenges of health, her resolve remains unshaken. Every morning, she rises with the same determination she had in the 1960s.

This store is not just a business; it is her sanctuary. You might find her taking a short rest within the store—a general sleeping on the battlefield. She refuses to leave her post.

She stays until evening. She ensures the books are balanced. She proves that retirement is a concept for those who lack passion.

Current Status: Working Daily at New Nyanza
Age: A testament to purpose over years
Philosophy: Work as medicine, consistency as king

Family & Lineage

The Three Pillars of Legacy

The Statesman: Adan Hersi

The influence of Mama Hersi’s household extended beyond commerce and into public life through Adan Hersi. Serving in government, Adan carried forward the family’s deeply rooted values of integrity, discipline, and responsibility into the sphere of public service. His work represents the Diplomatic Pillar of the family legacy—demonstrating how the principles shaped at New Nyanza could be applied to national leadership and governance.

The Industrialist: Amina Hersi Moghe

Amina Hersi Moghe transformed the entrepreneurial foundation laid in the 1950s into one of East Africa’s most recognisable business success stories. From early family enterprises in Bungoma to regional investments across Uganda and beyond, her journey reflects resilience, strategic vision, and long-term thinking. She stands as living proof of her mother’s philosophy—that determination, discipline, and clarity of purpose can turn local enterprise into regional influence.

The Philanthropist: Asha Hersi

Asha Hersi represents the humanitarian heart of the family legacy. Closely connected to her mother and the roots of New Nyanza, she has carried that sense of responsibility into structured community service through the Asha Hersi Foundation. Her work focuses on supporting vulnerable individuals and strengthening community dignity in Bungoma and surrounding areas. Through her efforts, the family’s success remains firmly anchored to service and social responsibility.

Her Legacy Lives On

Mama Hersi’s values continue through her children, grandchildren, and extended family, many of whom carry forward her principles in business, service, and community leadership. This legacy is not frozen in time. It is active, lived, and ongoing.

Community Impact

Her Work in the Community

Mama Hersi was known for:

  • Running businesses that ensured food access for working families
  • Supporting women with goods, credit, and opportunity when formal systems excluded them
  • Feeding people quietly, without publicity or condition
  • Acting as a point of stability in times of hardship
  • Many who benefited never asked, and many never forgot

In an age of noise and visibility, Mama Hersi’s life reminds us that the most important work is often done quietly. Her story stands as a reminder that real impact is measured in lives touched, not titles held.
